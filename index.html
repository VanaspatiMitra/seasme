About the Crop:

Sesamum - T-6 is a high-yielding variety known for its oil-rich seeds and drought tolerance. It grows best in well-drained, sandy loam to loamy soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. The crop requires approximately 300-500 mm of water per acre, which can be managed through rainfall or irrigation. Typically, T-6 yields around 400-600 kg of sesame seeds per acre. Profit margins are generally favorable, ranging from 15-25%, depending on market prices and production costs.

  • Soil Composition: well-drained loamy soils with a pH range of 6.0 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 0.98 to 1.64 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Soil Degradation, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Diammonium Phosphate, Farmyard Manure, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Carbaryl ,Furadan, Thiamethoxam.
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹48,000 to ₹1,08,000
